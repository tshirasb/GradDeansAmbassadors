    How do I register for classes?

    Drexel Connect > DrexelOne > Academics > Registration subsection has a button "Register for Courses"

    Check course availability, schedule ahead, and then easily register based on your planned schedule.

    How do I pick courses?

    You can find your major's degree requirements and sample plans of study at the CCI course catalog.

    Quarter System

    How rushed is it?

    Each quarter is about 10 weeks. Sounds fast? It is! The good news is, Drexel courses are carefully designed to cover 10 weeks of material. You're not cramming a semester into 10 weeks!

    How do I prepare?

    Three words to keep in mind:

    • Planning
      • Keep a calendar of important due dates
      • Track your weekly assignments
      • Get your textbooks and materials
    • Connecting
      • Be present!
      • Attend class and office hours
      • Participate in discussions
      • Ask questions early and often
    • Pacing
      • Schedule time wisely
      • Take breaks
      • Be proud of your accomplishments!

    Campus

    Is it safe?

    Drexel is located in the University City area in Philadelphia. Philly is a big city, so you should always be aware of your surroundings. Drexel has several safety precautions in place:

    • Police
      • Drexel campus police, aka Drexel Public Safety
      • UPENN campus police
      • Philadelphia police
    • Drexel Public Safety services
      • Save their number: 215.895.2222
    • Blue Light emergency phone system around campus
    • Drexel Guardian app
      • Contact Drexel Public Safety
      • Get a walking escort

    How do I get around?

    Check the CAMPUS tab for maps and shuttle schedules.

    What's fun in Philly?

    Philly is a great city for foodies, sports fans, and artists. There's something for everyone here, so get your SEPTA transit card and explore!

    Jobs and CO-OP

    How do I find a CO-OP?

    Fall intake students with eligible majors can do co-op. You can learn more about graduate co-op over at the Steinbright Career Development Center. Co-op students use the Drexel job search database to apply for co-op positions.

    How do I find a job?

    On the Steinbright Career Development Center site, you can find postings for on-campus jobs.

    Keep Steinbright in mind: their career counselors, alumni network, career events, and Handshake site will all help you find a full-time job after graduation!
